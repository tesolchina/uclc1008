## Slide 6
### In-text Citations in a Glance
### Direct Quote
### Author prominent (Narrative approach)
According to Jones (1998), "students often had difficulty using APA style, especially when it was their first time" (p. 199).
### Information prominent (Parenthetical approach)
- She states, "Students often had difficulty using APA style, especially when it was their first time" (Jones, 1998, p. 199).
### Paraphrasing
### Author prominent (Narrative approach)
Jones (1998) observes that students who first tried using APA style often found the experience challenging.
### Information prominent (Parenthetical approach)
- Students who first tried using APA style often found the experience challenging (Jones, 1998) .

## Slide 16
### In-text Citations: Source text with multiple authors
### 1. Two Authors
Tudor-Locke and Bassett (2004) contend that pedometers are simple and inexpensive hardware.
Pedometers are simple and inexpensive hardware (Tudor-Locke & Bassett, 2004).
### 2. Three or more Authors
If the information that you want to cite originates from three or more authors, mention the first author and et al. in every citation including the first citation. For example:
First and subsequent citations:
### Krug et al. (2000) argue that…
### … (Krug et al., 2000)

## Slide 18
### In-text Citations: Citing a secondary source
When you want to “borrow” an idea mentioned by another author who is cited in the paper that you are reading.
### Secondary source: Hasinoff (2016)
### the article you are reading
### Primary source: Trottier (2012)
### cited in the paper you read
### With signal phrases
### Trottier (2012, as cited in Hasinoff, 2016) reported that …
### Without signal phrases
### earlier article appears first

## Slide 19
### In-text Citations: Citing a secondary source
Key reminders:
Follow chronological order. The article published earlier (primary source) will appear BEFORE the article published later.
Provide an entry for the secondary source in the reference list.
Use secondary sources sparingly.
### earlier article appears first

## Slide 30
### End-of-text Citations (aka References/ Bibliography)
### End-of-text citations appear after the writing
### Hanging Indentation
### Sequenced alphabetically
### Italicize the Title of periodical and Volume number
Use a full-stop to separate each piece of information. A full-stop is NOT necessary after a doi or hyperlink

### E.g. https://scholar.google.com/
