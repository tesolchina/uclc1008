## Slide 3
### Assessment Structure ofCritical Response to Academic Arguments (20%)
### In-class: Week 13: Nov 24-28
### Duration: 6 minutes per studentPart 1: Listening & Note-Taking (2 minutes)
Students listen to a recording (including a narration and the claim) and may take notes.
### Part 2: Preparation (2 minutes)
Students prepare a summary and a refutation based on the recording.
### Part 3: Presentation (2 minutes)
Students deliver their summary (1st minute of your response) and refutation (2nd minute of your response) .
Topic Selection:
10 broad topics will be prepared, each with a positive and negative claim (totaling 20 possible questions).
Students will randomly draw a question on the spot.

---

## Slide 4
You will listen to excerpts from group discussions addressing current academic topics. Each discussion will begin with relevant background information, including the topic’s context and key definitions, and a brief description of the central controversies surrounding the topic. Your tasks are to:
### 1st Half of your response
### Summarise the speaker's position and claim with accuracy
### 2nd Half of your response
Develop and present a counterargument that identifies logical or evidentiary limitations in the speaker’s claim.
