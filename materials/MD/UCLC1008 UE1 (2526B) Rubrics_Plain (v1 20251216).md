## Academic Tone & Clarity (20%)

**Formality, Clarity, Flow of Ideas and Cohesion**

| 19-18-17-16 | 15-14-13-12 | 11-10-9-8-7 |
|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| **Formal tone with no informal language** (e.g., contractions, colloquialisms, or conversational phrasing) | **Predominantly formal but may include some lapses** (e.g., a contraction, slightly informal word choice) | **Frequent use of informal phrasing** (e.g., conversational language, slang, or overuse of first-person pronouns) |
| Sentences are clear, logically structured, and free of ambiguity | Sentences are generally logically structured | Ideas may be hard to follow as sentences lack logical connections |
| Smooth transitions between ideas with no awkward phrasing | Some transitions may be abrupt or mechanical | Awkward or disjointed transitions are evident |
| Provides a well-structured summary with a clear thesis statement in the introduction, an effective topic sentence for each body paragraph, and a relevant conclusion | Thesis statement is present but may be imprecise | Thesis statement is vague or off-topic |
|  | Topic sentences are clear but may not fully align with the thesis or lack development | Topic sentences are weak or disconnected from the thesis |
|  | Conclusion summarises a few points only | Conclusion is repetitive or does not summarise key points |

#### 7 and below

- Overly casual, inappropriate for academic writing (e.g., text-message style, excessive slang)
- Ideas are consistently unclear or confusing due to absence of logical structure
- Ideas are disjointed due to absence of transitions
- Thesis statement is missing or incoherent
- Topic sentences are absent or unrelated to the excerpts
- Conclusion is missing or does not address the topic
