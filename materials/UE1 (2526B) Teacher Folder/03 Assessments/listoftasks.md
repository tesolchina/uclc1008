# ASSESSMENTS

## 1. Academic Writing Quiz (15%)
- **CILOs**: 1, 2, 3
- **In-class**: 23-27 Feb
- **Description**: Students will analyze academic texts to summarize, synthesize, and paraphrase key information, ensuring academic tone and proper citations.
- **Input**: Two journal articles
- **Task**: Summarize and synthesize
- **Length**: No more than 300 words
- **Format**: Writing on paper (60 minutes)

## 2. Argument Construction and Evaluation (Draft) (15%)
- **CILOs**: 1, 2, 3
- **In-class**: 16-20 Mar
- **Description**: Students will write a 400-500 word draft based on academic articles, using a specific argumentation model to construct an argument, counterargument, and rebuttal. It emphasizes selecting ideas, supporting evidence, and familiarizing with argumentation frameworks (e.g., Toulmin Model, Rogerian Argument).
- **Sample**: `/Users/simonwang/Documents/Usage/UE1/materials/UE1 (2526B) Teacher Folder/03 Assessments/UE1 (2526a) Sample ACE Draft (Student).pdf`

## 3. Argument Construction and Evaluation (Final) (25%)
- **CILOs**: 1, 2, 3, 5
- **Due**: 1 May
- **This assessment is split into two parts:**

### 3a. Final Paper (20%)
- **Description**: Students will incorporate feedback from teachers, AI tools, and peers to extend their draft to approximately 800 words. The process involves evaluating arguments, counterarguments, and rebuttals to construct a well-supported and persuasive argument.
- **Format**: Take-home assignment

### 3b. Reflection on AI Use (5%)
- **Description**: Students must reflect on their process of working with AI and critique AI-generated material, justifying changes made in the final paper.

## 4. Critical Response to Academic Arguments (20%)
- **CILOs**: 1, 2, 4
- **In-class**: 20-24 Apr
- **Description**: Students will listen to an audio clip with background information and an argument, then prepare and present a verbal response summarizing and rebutting the argument.
- **Format**: Speaking (in-class)
- **Topics**: `/Users/simonwang/Documents/Usage/UE1/materials/UE1 (2526B) Teacher Folder/03 Assessments/UCLC1008 UE1 (2526a) Topics for CRAA.pdf`

## 5. Reflective Learning Portfolio (10%)
- **CILO**: 5
- **Due**: 1 May - **NO LATE submission**
- **Description**: Students will engage in a 500-word reflective analysis of two to three learning episodes involving AI tools. They must critically compare their original work with AI-generated versions, explain their rationale for adoption or rejection, and document interactions with AI tools. The goal is to help students use AI as a learning partner and enhance self-directed learning skills.

## 6. Class Participation (15%)
- **CILOs**: 1, 2, 3, 4
- **This assessment includes:**

### 6a. Students' Participation and Involvement (5%)
- **Description**: Participation and involvement in in- and out-of-class activities
- **Duration**: Whole semester

### 6b. Peer Evaluation on Argument Construction and Evaluation (Draft) (5%)
- **In-class**: 13-17 Apr
- **Description**: Peer evaluation of draft arguments

### 6c. Pre-course Writing (2.5%)
- **Due**: 23 Jan - **NO LATE submission**

### 6d. Referencing Quiz (2.5%)
- **Due**: 30 Jan - **NO LATE submission**
