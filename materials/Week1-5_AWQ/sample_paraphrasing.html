1. Warm-up: Understanding Paraphrasing Concepts WARM-UP

Let's start with some multiple-choice questions to gently introduce key concepts about paraphrasing.

Question 1: What is the main purpose of paraphrasing?

  • A) To copy the original text exactly
  • B) To restate ideas in your own words while maintaining the original meaning
  • C) To change the meaning of the original text
  • D) To make the text shorter

Question 2: Which paraphrasing strategy involves replacing words with similar meanings?

  • A) Using synonyms
  • B) Changing word forms
  • C) Changing voice
  • D) Changing sentence patterns

Question 3: Which example demonstrates changing from active to passive voice?

Original: The students completed the assignment successfully.

  • A) The assignment was completed successfully by the students.
  • B) The pupils finished the task successfully.
  • C) Students successfully completed assignments.
  • D) The assignment completion was successful by students.

Question 4: What should you always do after paraphrasing someone else's work?

  • A) Nothing - paraphrasing means you don't need to cite
  • B) Cite the original source
  • C) Change more words
  • D) Make it longer

Question 5: Which is the best paraphrase of this sentence?

Original: Many educators believe that technology enhances learning outcomes.

  • A) Many educators believe that technology enhances learning outcomes.
  • B) Numerous teachers think that technology improves educational results.
  • C) Technology enhances learning outcomes, many educators believe.
  • D) Many educators believe technology enhances learning outcomes.
Key Concept: Paraphrasing involves restating ideas in your own words while maintaining the original meaning. It's not just about changing a few words - you need to understand the concept and express it differently.
