Before You Practise: What Good Paraphrasing Looks Like

As an academic writer, you paraphrase to integrate sources smoothly while showing understanding. A strong paraphrase changes both language and structure without changing meaning, and it still needs a citation.

Behaviour goal: When you paraphrase, you read → identify key meaning → re-express in a new structure → check accuracy → cite.

Four core strategies (use 2+ together)

  • Synonyms: replace key words with accurate alternatives (not every word)
  • Word forms: change noun ↔ verb, adjective ↔ noun, etc.
  • Voice: active ↔ passive when appropriate
  • Sentence patterns: split/merge clauses, change order, use different connectors

Worked demo

Original: The research demonstrates that technology significantly impacts education.
Step 1 (meaning): Technology has a large effect on education, according to research.
Step 2 (rewrite): Research suggests that educational outcomes are strongly influenced by technology.
Step 3 (cite): Research suggests that educational outcomes are strongly influenced by technology (Author, Year).

1. Warm-up: Understanding Paraphrasing Concepts WARM-UP

Let's start with some multiple-choice questions to gently introduce key concepts about paraphrasing.

Question 1: What is the main purpose of paraphrasing?

  • A) To copy the original text exactly
  • B) To restate ideas in your own words while maintaining the original meaning
  • C) To change the meaning of the original text
  • D) To make the text shorter
Explanation: Paraphrasing is about demonstrating understanding. Copying (A) is plagiarism. Changing meaning (C) creates inaccuracy. Making text shorter (D) is summarizing, which is different from paraphrasing.

Question 2: Which paraphrasing strategy involves replacing words with similar meanings?

  • A) Using synonyms
  • B) Changing word forms
  • C) Changing voice
  • D) Changing sentence patterns
Explanation: Synonyms are different words with the same meaning (e.g., 'important' = 'significant'). Changing word forms (B) involves grammar (e.g., noun to verb). Voice (C) and sentence patterns (D) involve structural changes.

Question 3: Which example demonstrates changing from active to passive voice?

Original: The students completed the assignment successfully.

  • A) The assignment was completed successfully by the students.
  • B) The pupils finished the task successfully.
  • C) Students successfully completed assignments.
  • D) The assignment completion was successful by students.

Question 4: What should you always do after paraphrasing someone else's work?

  • A) Nothing - paraphrasing means you don't need to cite
  • B) Cite the original source
  • C) Change more words
  • D) Make it longer

Question 5: Which is the best paraphrase of this sentence?

Original: Many educators believe that technology enhances learning outcomes.

  • A) Many educators believe that technology enhances learning outcomes.
  • B) Numerous teachers think that technology improves educational results.
  • C) Technology enhances learning outcomes, many educators believe.
  • D) Many educators believe technology enhances learning outcomes.
Explanation: Option B is best because it changes vocabulary ('educators' → 'teachers', 'enhances' → 'improves') and structure while keeping the exact meaning. Option A is a copy. Option C is awkward. Option D is too close to the original.
Key Concept: Paraphrasing involves restating ideas in your own words while maintaining the original meaning. It's not just about changing a few words - you need to understand the concept and express it differently.

2. Try It Yourself: Short Answer Practice

Now try paraphrasing these sentences on your own. Don't worry if you're not sure - just give it your best attempt! You'll receive AI feedback to help you improve.

Strategy Focus: Synonyms
Replace words with others that have the same meaning in this context.
Example: "The results were good." → "The results were positive."
Caution: Check that the synonym fits the academic style (e.g., avoid slang).

Task 1: Paraphrase using synonyms

Original: The research demonstrates that technology significantly impacts education.
Words: 0
Sample: Research suggests that technology has a substantial effect on education (Author, Year).
Strategy Focus: Word Forms
Change the part of speech (Noun ↔ Verb ↔ Adjective). This often forces you to change the sentence structure around the word.
Example: "The creation of the policy..." (Noun) → "They created the policy..." (Verb)

Task 2: Paraphrase by changing word forms

Original: The implementation of new policies requires careful consideration.
Words: 0
Sample: Introducing new policies requires decision-makers to consider the implications carefully (Author, Year).
Strategy Focus: Voice (Active ↔ Passive)
Switch between "Who did it" (Active) and "What was done" (Passive).
Active: "The government passed the law."
Passive: "The law was passed by the government."

Task 3: Paraphrase by changing voice (active to passive)

Original: Researchers conducted extensive studies on this topic.
Words: 0
Sample: Extensive studies were carried out on this topic by researchers (Author, Year).

Task 4: Paraphrase by changing sentence patterns

Original: Although the results were promising, further research is needed.
Words: 0
Sample: Further research is still necessary despite the encouraging results (Author, Year).

3. Advanced Practice: Combining Multiple Strategies

Now try paraphrasing longer sentences using multiple strategies at once.

Task 5: Paraphrase using multiple strategies

Original: The investigation revealed significant findings about student performance that educators should consider when developing new teaching methods.
Words: 0
Sample: The study identified important results on student performance, which educators should take into account when designing new teaching approaches (Author, Year).
Challenge: Complex Sentences
Long sentences often have multiple clauses. Break them down: identify the main idea first, then rewrite it simply, then add the details back in your own structure.

Task 6: Paraphrase a complex sentence

Original: Many universities have implemented online learning platforms because they recognize that digital education can provide flexible learning opportunities for students who have various scheduling constraints.
Words: 0
Sample: Universities have adopted online platforms because they acknowledge that digital education can offer flexible options for students with different time constraints (Author, Year).
